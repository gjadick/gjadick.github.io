Developing a realistic CT simulator
============


I worked at Ravin Advanced Imaging (RAI) Labs at Duke University for a year, beginning as a summer research fellow and then as a research technician, where I was advised by Ehsan Abadi and Ehsan Samei. I helped to develop DukeSim, a hybrid CT simulator comprised of different modules such as a raytracing forward-projection and Monte-Carlo scatter estimation. I implemented several features, including a polynomial-based beam hardening correction, tube current modulation algorithm, spherical detector geometry, and photon counting detector correlated noise model. I additionally wrote a Python wrapper script that integrated the different modules of DukeSim and facilitated rapid simulations for virtual imaging trials.

As a result of this work, I presented at SPIE Medical Imaging 2021, [published in *Physics in Medicine and Biology*](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1361-6560/ac2269/meta) in 2021, and co-authored several conference proceedings and an article recently published in *Chest*.
