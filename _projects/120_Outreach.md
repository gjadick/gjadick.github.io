<!-- Medical physics community outreach
============ -->

I have been very involved in community outreach throughout my time as an undergraduate at Duke and now during my PhD at UofC. As an undergrad, I was president of our chapter of the Society of Physics Students for two years, where I organized and participated in our outreach efforts. Now at UofC, I am co-student director of our graduate program's Diversity & Outreach Committee, through which I have developed interactive demos, [organized a professional learning day](https://medphys.uchicago.edu/news-archive/gpmp-do-outreach-committee-hosts-cps-physics-school-teacher-professional-development) for Chicago Public Schools (CPS) teachers, and am currently working on another CPS collaboration.

The demos I have developed:
* [Wave physics and MRI](https://github.com/miramliu/SouthSideScience_Fair/tree/main/MRI) - demonstrating how the principles of 1D acoustic waves produced by a guitar provide intuition into the 2D EM waves utilized to generate MR images. (code provided by M Liu)

* [CT image reconstruction](https://github.com/gjadick/interactive-CT-recon) - interactive virtual CT scanner with rotating x-ray tube, illustrating the process of filtered backprojection reconstruction.
