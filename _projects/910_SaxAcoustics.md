MicroCT scanning, digitally analyzing, and 3D printing vintage saxophone mouthpieces
============

In my 3rd and 4th years at Duke, I worked with Joshua Socolar and Matthew Busch on the Bass Connections team, “[The Art and Craft of Saxophone Mouthpiece Design](https://bassconnections.duke.edu/virtual-showcase/archiving-saxophone-mouthpiece-designs).” We used micro-CT scanning to [digitally archive](https://maxbartlett.shinyapps.io/project/) rare vintage tenor saxophone mouthpieces. My niche on the research team was audio analysis, breaking down audio recordings from eight musicians playing on a variety of mouthpieces and quantifying the spectral metrics of each. We were able to use our digital archive to faithfully replicate vintage mouthpieces using 3D printing, which has now spun off into the GS Mouthpieces series. [Our poster won runner-up](https://bassconnections.duke.edu/sites/bassconnections.duke.edu/files/site-images/art-craft-saxophone-mouthpiece-design.pdf) in the 2020 Bass Connections poster competition.
